Pick a night, scrub the time, and watch the Moon, planets and bright stars trace their paths across the sky.

Solid dots mark positions at the selected time; faint trails show each object’s path through the night (sunset to sunrise). The Moon is drawn with its true crescent orientation. Azimuth runs west-to-east the way you’d scan the horizon; altitude is height above it.
